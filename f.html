Understanding and Treating Scalp Psoriasis with Ayurveda

In a world where 125 million people suffer from psoriasis, it becomes necessary to know the root cause of this disease and how to cure it permanently. Like any other skin disease, psoriasis can reach the scalp and create issues that impact your self-confidence.

Scalp psoriasis is a persistent skin condition of the scalp characterized by red, flaky patches. Those who have it experience itching, pain, and discomfort and even feel ashamed of their appearance. This leaves the patient wondering ‘how to cure scalp psoriasis permanently,’ and the answer lies in our ancient system of medicine - Ayurveda!

Symptoms of Scalp Psoriasis:

Scalp psoriasis may range from mild to severe. Common symptoms include:

  • Red patches on the scalp: These patches can be pink or reddish on lighter skin tones or purple or brown on darker skin tones.
  • Silvery-white scales (plaques): Usually thickened but may be removed quite easily, causing bleeding at times.
  • Itchy scalp: It can be very irritating and uncomfortable at times, leading to itchiness that causes extreme discomfort.
  • Scalp dryness: The scalp feels tight and uncomfortable.
  • Burning or soreness: There may be a hot or tender feeling on the scalp sometimes.
  • Hair loss: A temporary hair loss can occur in some severe cases.

Psoriasis Treatment in Ayurveda

In Ayurveda, kitibha or ekakushtha are the names given to psoriasis, which is considered a type of kushta (skin disease). It happens because there is vitiation in Vata-Kapha dosha, leading to the accumulation of toxins (Ama) in the body. Poor eating habits, stress, unhealthy lifestyles, or inheritance might make this problem worse. ‘Psoriasis treatment in Ayurveda’ involves detoxification, dietary changes, herbal remedies, and lifestyle modifications.

The Root Causes of Psoriasis

Scalp psoriasis is mainly caused by an imbalance of Vata and Kapha doshas. It is characterized by scaly patches and dry scalp that are often accompanied by itching and discomfort due to being roughened, hardened (drying) by the wind (Vata), or moistened with Kapha.

Ayurveda considers unhealthy lifestyle choices, poor nutrition, stress, and toxins as factors that intensify these imbalances, thus leading to skin infections like scalp psoriasis.

How To Cure Scalp Psoriasis Permanently with Ayurveda?

Detoxification (Panchakarma)

Panchakarma is a fundamental part of treating psoriasis, according to Ayurveda. This process consists of various curative techniques designed to cleanse the body of toxins while also balancing out the Doshas (Vata, Pitta, Kapha). Some key Panchakarma therapies for scalp psoriasis include:

  • Vamana (Therapeutic Vomiting): This helps remove excess Kapha dosha and accumulated ama (toxins) from your system.
  • Virechana (Purgation): It purges out heat from the liver and intestines by getting pitta dosha out through passing stool.
  • Basti (Enema Therapy): This type of therapy removes vata dosha imbalance so as to detoxify the colon. The process involves inserting medicated liquid into the rectum to help you pass the stool smoothly.
  • Raktamokshana (Bloodletting): This technique eliminates harmful substances from the bloodstream, reducing inflammation. It usually involves precision methods to suck out the impure blood with the use of instruments or sometimes leeches.
Dietary Modifications (Ahara)

Now, if you’re wondering how to cure scalp psoriasis permanently while not getting into critical therapies, Ayurveda has simple ways, too! It focuses on the root cause of every disease, which is an unhealthy diet in most cases. Scalp psoriasis can be effectively managed with the help of personalized dietary interventions. Ayurveda advocates for a Sattvic diet that is light, easy to digest, and free from inflammatory foods. Some important dietary guidelines are:

  • Avoidance of Trigger Foods: Do not eat spicy, salty, or sour food that increases pitta dosha in your system. Stay away from processed foods, white sugars, and deep-fried items that increase Kapha dosha.
  • Inclusion of Fresh, Whole Foods: Eat fresh fruits, vegetables, whole grains and legumes. Go for cooling fruits like cucumber, watermelon, and coconut water.
  • Herbal Teas: Make herbal teas using neem leaves, turmeric powder, or tulsi leaves, which help reduce inflammation and purify blood.
  • Adequate Hydration: Drink a sufficient amount of water throughout the day to remove toxins from the body and remain hydrated.
Herbal Remedies (Aushadhi)

Some powerful anti-inflammatory herbs are present in Ayurveda that can be used to manage scalp psoriasis effectively. These encompass:

  • Azadirachta indica (Neem): It is characterized by potent antibacterial and antifungal properties. You can apply neem oil or paste on the scalp to alleviate redness and itchy feeling.
  • Curcuma longa (Turmeric): It is a natural anti-inflammatory agent. Rub a mixture of this herb with coconut oil onto your head or alternatively use turmeric with yogurt, once a week.
  • Aloe Vera: Aloe vera gel calms down the scalp while eliminating redness and scaling. Apply pure aloe vera gel topically at the affected sites.
  • Rubia cordifolia (Manjistha): Manjishta helps cleanse the blood, thereby reducing inflammation. To use manjistha powder - mix it with lukewarm water and consume orally or you can also apply it locally.
  • Commiphora mukul (Guggulu): It is a popular anti-inflammatory and detoxifying herb. One can take it in tablet form under an Ayurvedic doctor’s recommendation.
Lepa or Topical Applications

Topical applications of Ayurvedic formulas can help to relieve the symptoms of scalp psoriasis.

  • Ayurvedic Oils: To reduce dryness and scaling, massage into the scalp with oils such as Karanja oil, Brahmi oil, or Jatyadi oil.
  • Herbal Pastes: A paste made from sandalwood, turmeric, and neem powder mixed with water or rose water can be applied.
  • Coconut Oil: Regular application of coconut oil moisturizes the scalp and may also reduce itching and scaling.
Vihara or Lifestyle Modifications

Ayurveda gives prominence to the balanced lifestyle of people in dealing with Scalp Psoriasis. Some of these include:

  • Stress Management: Practice yoga, meditation, and pranayama for managing stress levels and promoting good mental health. Stress is a well-known trigger for psoriasis flare-ups.
  • Regular Exercise: Walking, swimming, or cycling are all forms of moderate exercises that can improve blood circulation and one’s overall health.
  • Adequate Sleep: Ensure you sleep 7-8 hours every night to aid the body heal itself.
  • Sun Exposure: A little sunlight every day helps reduce psoriasis symptoms. Do this daily for about 10-15 minutes.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the causes of scalp psoriasis?

An overactive immune system is believed to be the scientific cause of scalp psoriasis. Genetic factors also play a role.

Can stress trigger scalp psoriasis flare-ups?

Stress is the root cause of many diseases including Psoriasis. The skin has a direct connection with our mental health. So managing stress is important for controlling this condition.

Is scalp psoriasis contagious?

No, scalp psoriasis is not contagious.

How can I prevent scalp psoriasis flare-ups?

Managing stress, avoiding harsh hair products, and following your Ayurvedic treatment plan can help prevent flare-ups.

Does scalp psoriasis cause hair loss?

Yes, scalp psoriasis can lead to hair loss because of the following reasons:

  • Scratching: The intense itching caused by psoriasis can lead to damaged hair follicles, causing hair fall.
  • Inflammation: The inflammation associated with psoriasis can weaken hair follicles, making them more vulnerable to shedding.
  • Scale buildup: Thick, scaly patches can also clog hair follicles, hindering their growth at times.
